All Saints Church, Woolley

Architect: John Wood the Younger

Date: 1761

Description:

All Saints Church, Woolley is situated just four miles north west of Bath.

The Church was built on the site of an earlier chapel in 1761 by John Wood the Younger. It was paid for by Mrs Elizabeth Parkin who was the owner of the nearby gunpowder factory in Woolley. 

For more information about the Gunpowder Factory

It is also the final resting place of Admiral Puget who surveyed Puget Sound in the Pacific North West America near Seattle that bears his name.

In the distance the Church of St Mary in Upper Swainswick which is the resting place of his father John Wood the Elder.

Thorpe’s Map of Bath 1743 (below), showing the previous church at Woolley, the Gunpowder Factory at Woolley and also St Mary’s Church in Upper Swainswick.
